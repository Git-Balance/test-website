Half Life: Opposing Force

I bought a bunch of Half Life Games in a bundle a while ago. I decided to start playing Opposing Force, when I came across something that relates to what we are learning.

The Tram

There is a tram in Opposing Force that you have to ride to advance in the game. It is a great example of how to use metal.

Metal is

Ductile

Metal can be made into wires. Wires are bendable, but are capable of carrying a lot of weight. This makes them great for carrying a tram, since wires are able to bend in ways that materials like wood are simply incapable of.

Malleable

Metal can be bent or hammered into a specific shape without breaking. This lets people create strong parts relatively easily, like the walls and floor of the tram.

Conducts Electricity

Metal travels through electricity easily. This allows signals like turn left, play message 3, or EMERGENCY BREAK to be sent easily and quickly through electricity.
